The Amazon rainforest, and the river basin are home for many distinctive life forms and Botos are one of them. Boto is a Portuguese name given to several types of dolphins and river dolphins native to the Amazon and the Orinoco River tributaries.

Pink River Dolphins - Botos

These extrodanary looking aquatic mamals are living in rivers and lakes, like flood waters in Amazon. Water in these flooded forest are tanned by the vegetation. Botos have specialist weponary to hunt in dark waters.

This near real time population counting application records number of botto observations with location and time. see below the map for map controls.

The botos are a paraphyletic group, defined largely by their evolutionary convergences. The genus Sotalia is divided into two species. The costero (S. guianensis) is distributed in the Atlantic, from Florianópolis in Santa Catarina, Brazil, and northwards. The tucuxi (S. fluviatilis) lives in the rivers of the Amazon.

why are they pink?
"The coloring is believed to be scar tissue from rough games or fighting over conquests. The brighter the pink, the more attractive the males are to females—at least during mating season, which takes place when the water has receded and males and females are confined to the river channel again." - National Geographics
